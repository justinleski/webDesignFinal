Data

The Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is a key component in many facets of Computer Science. It allows for simultaneous computing which is essential for crunching large amounts of data. Data is beneficial for machine learning algorithms and especially for neural networks. By processing large amounts of data, AI uses it to identify patterns such as in populations, the weather, or visually recognizable patterns such as textiles. All of these examples and more can be use cases of AI, and it goes beyond that. AI is so versatile, that it can be implemented into virtually any piece of software and industry. If it should be is another discussion, yet the sheer number of AI-related products being produced is indicative of a paradigm shift.

Generative AI

Another area where AI excels is in generation, most notably of images. Recent advancements in generative models like DALL-E have proven that creating something from almost nothing is now possible with AI. By inserting a prompt, the model will take the keywords provided and begin to map together the concepts and how they may interact and produces an image based on the relevant keywords and prompts. In fact, a man by the name of Jason Allen entered an art competition using an image created by AI. Subsequently, he won and received a prize for the art in spite of not creating it himself (Kuta). It goes without saying that creating such complex pieces that they may win an art competition requires a good amount of computing power. Hence, these models are very intensive in their usage of computation which necessitates the GPU. It is an impressive marvel of technology, demonstrating the power of the GPU.
